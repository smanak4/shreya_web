I WANT

7 rings

I WANT IT, I GOT IT

My Favorite Things is a classic song that your middle school music teacher will make you sing regardless of skill level. It’s likely because it is a simple song that expresses the beauties of life even when you get a horrible allergic reaction from a bee sting. However, a former Victorious actress changes the entire theme of focusing on the happiness brought from being a consumer, specifically a wealthy consumer.

While My Favorite Things reminisces on good times in contrast to bad times. 7 rings on “wanting” good times and especially things. In fact 7 rings focuses more on the things compared to My Favorite Things. Grande gets what she wants because she is a girlboss living in a capitalist society. In order for people to have their favorite things in the 21st century they have to get what they want, not necessarily what they need. The song expresses money in a positive light, so “wanting” more materialistic items represents the power money gives to a person. Grande can get what she wants because she already has what she needs.

In terms of reflecting the reality of society, most people cannot afford to want something and simply get it. In fact, according to the United States Census 37.2 million people live in poverty in the US alone. However, in a world ruled by social media there are constant subliminal and direct advertisements that make you believe you want something and can afford to get it. People are constantly consuming material things even if they don’t necessarily need it. While Grande can express power in getting what she wants, she does not express the downfalls of constant consumption. Grande’s song is just a lovely dedication to our capitalistic society, and I can appreciate it.
